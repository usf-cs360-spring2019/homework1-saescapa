Prototypes

Visualization 1

Number of incidents by hour of the day. The data consists of incidents for the period of December 2018, with 12,281 results. The number of incidents increases particularily during the early morning, at 11AM. The total number of incidents is relatively consistent throughout the mid-day until 7PM, to which point there is a large decrease of incidents. Samuel Escapa - CS 360

Interpretation

This graph represents the number of incidents by a particular time of the day for the month of December 2018.

Discussion

The pattern of this graph shows a large increase of incidents at around 11am. As thought otherwise, most incidents occur in the middle of the day, and drops starting at 6PM. At 2AM until 6AM, the number of incidents are at its lowest. This pattern could be explained by the simple fact that there are less people in the streets during the day, and therefore less chances of incidents occuring.

Credit

Data set: SFPD Incidents 2018-2019
Simple Line graph: d3noob

Visualization 2

Count of Incidents for location intersections with over 30 incidents. The data consists of incidents for the period of December 2018, with 1,361 results. This represents locations with 1 or more incidents on average per day every month. Powell St / OFarrell Street in particular accounts for 13% of the incidents in this subset. Samuel Escapa - CS 360

Interpretation

This graph displays the number of incidents for each intersection with 30 or more incidents, meaning, averaging one incident or more per day for the month of December 2018. The gradient represents the count of incidents, darker representing a larger amount in comparision to the lowest count, 30.

Discussion

There is a particular intersection, Powell and O'Farell, which accounts for rougly 15% of the data presented. This intersection in particular is a busy touristy area with shopping stores, but it is also right on the outside of one of the most dangerous neighborhoods, the Tenderloin. The combination of both large amount of tourist and the entrance of Tenderloin results in large amounts of incidents. With this knowledge, we should expect in the next graph that the largest incident is personal theft.

Credit

Data set: SFPD Incidents 2018-2019
Simple bar chart: Mike Bostock
Linear gradient chart: EfratVil

Visualization 3

Different types of incidents and count of them for the intersection of Powell ST / O'Farrell St. The data consists of incidents for the period of December 2018, with 180 results. As clearly seen, Larceny Theft accounts for 50% of the incidents in the most incidental intersection in San Francisco. Samuel Escapa - CS 360

Interpretation

This graph displays the different types of incidents in the intersection with the largest amount of incidents, Powell/O'Farell.

Discussion

There is a clear and overwhelming incident type which is Larceny/Theft, accounting for 50% of the incidents in this intersection. This makes sense as this intersection is very touristy, which has a lot of traffic and is right in the outskirt of the Tenderloin.

Credit

Data set: SFPD Incidents 2018-2019
